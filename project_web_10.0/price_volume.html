Section Summary Sales Volume and Price of Used Cars

The Used cars market is expanding these years. It is important to understand the sales volumes and price trend. This part combines various data features to visually explore the trends and patterns. With the help of effective visualization, it can give a comprehensive view of automobile sales from different perspectives.

This section answers the following subquestions:

  • What is the volume distribution by car features, such as brands, certificated, bodystyle, registeration year?
  • Who are the main participants of the used cars market?
  • What is the price trend for cars in different generation

Takeaways:

  1. American brands are dominating the used cars market.
  2. Most cars have been certified. SUV are most popular style.
  3. Majority of the sellers are dealers and most of them work with only one brand.
  4. Recent generation cars have higher price and the price distribution is more dispersed.

Packed Bubble Chart & Network D3
 Participants of the market

Packed Bubble Chart
The big blue bubble represents the number of dealers and the small orange one represents the number of individual sellers. There are 110,731 dealers and 12,817 individual sellers in the used cars market. Obviously, dealers are the major players.

Network D3
The network graph was created in R. Since this is a network, we have the following important elements:
Nodes: dealers with IDs and used cars brands
These nodes are colored according to their out-degree.
Edges: the connection between a dealer and a brand
If there exists an edge, it means that the dealer is selling used cars of that specific brand.
Weights: the sales volume
We only keep the sales volume which is bigger than 10 to downsize the dataset. Bolder edge represents larger sales volume.
We can see that Ford, Toyota and Chevrolet are the top 3 popular brands in the market.

Distribution Plot Price Distribution by Year

The analysis above gives an overall view of dynamic customized car price distribution. Surprisingly, the price distribution by year seems to have a certain pattern. To look into this specific problem, an overlaid price distribution graph by 4 different years is created.

Figure 2.6 Price Distribution by Year

code

From this graph, it is easy to find the used cars’ overall price is decreasing with the adding of the cars’ model year in recent 15 years. But we also have another interesting finding that the price range of used cars also becomes smaller with the adding of the cars’ model year in recent 15 years. We know the price range of new cars are similar in each year, so we can conclude that the depreciation rate of expensive cars is higher than the cheap cars made in the same year.

Distribution Plot Price Distribution by Mileage

The Dynamic Customized Car Price Distribution graph also shows the price distribution by mileage seems to have an interesting pattern. To look into this specific problem, an overlaid price distribution graph by 5 groups of mileage ranges is created.

Figure 2.7 Price Distribution by Mileage

code

From this graph we can easily find the overall price of used cars is decreasing with the increasing of the mileage. But we also find an interesting fact that the range of the used cars’ price is also closer with the increasing of the mileage. We know the price range of new cars are wide, but the range is closer and closer with the increasing of the mileage, which means the cars with a low mileage have a higher depreciation rate than the same car with a high mileage.
